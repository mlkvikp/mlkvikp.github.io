About Me

I am a final-year PhD student in the Materials Theory group at ETH Zürich, working with Prof. Nicola A. Spaldin and Prof. Claude Ederer. My research focuses on electronic phase transitions in correlated materials, combining condensed matter theory, materials science, and high-performance computing.
I enjoy solving complex problems through simulation and data, and I’m looking to apply these skills in data-driven or computational roles in industry.

Research Interests

I study metal-to-insulator transitions in correlated metal oxides such as vanadium dioxide, VO2. These materials exhibit rich electronic behavior and pose fundamental questions in condensed matter physics, with promising applications in low-power electronics and neuromorphic computing.
